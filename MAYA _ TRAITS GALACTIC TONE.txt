Mayan Astrology tends to be the most mysterious from the very beginning. Like the Indian, China and other civilizations who have their own astrology in the same way the Mayans too had their own astrology. But the Mayan astrology was very much different from any other astrological tradition. Before we have a clear concept about the Mayan astrology it is very important to know about the Mayan civilization. The Mayan civilization also thrived around thousands of years ago and among any other civilization they were the most advanced civilization.


The Mayan people had an intense bond between the earth and heaven. In the Mayan culture there were also very good astronomers who could predict eclipses and other celestial events accurately as they used to notice all the signs that were given by nature.  Their astronomical predictions were extremely accurate. The astronomers of the Maya very well understood the concept of time and invented the Mayan Calendar. The Mayan Calendar was also known as the Tzolk'in and it consisted of 20 day signs and 13 galactic numbers which gave a 260 day period calendar. Later the Tzolkin was combined with a 365-day vague solar year which is known as the Haab. The  Mayan Haab was mainly the Maya solar calendar which was made up of eighteen months of twenty days each plus a period of five days. The Mayans used to believe that one cycle ends and the new life cycle begins. With the concept of repeating cycles the Mayans introduced 19 zodiac signs. The Mayans kept the indigenous names of the zodiac sign according to the different planet, nature and animal but the Mayan zodiac sign has their source from the Mayan culture, weather, the four directions, and Mayan astronomy. At present in Mayan astrology there are 20 day signs with 13 galactic numbers and 19 zodiac signs.

        "Ahau", "Imix", "Ik", "Akbal", "Kan", "Chicchan", "Cimi", "Manik",

        "Caban", "Etznab", "Cauac"
    ]
    
    # Julian Day Number conversion
    a = (14 - month) // 12
    y = year + 4800 - a
    m = month + 12 * a - 3
    jd = day + (153 * m + 2) // 5 + 365 * y + y // 4 - y // 100 + y // 400 - 32045
    
    # Correlation constant (GMT)
    d = jd - 584283


    # Tone calculation (sacred AMA offset +3)
    tone = (d % 13 + 1 + 3) % 13 or 13


    # Glyph of the day
    glyph = glyphs[d % 20]


    # Trecena leader (first glyph of the 13-day cycle)
    trecena_start = d - ((tone - 1) % 13)
    trecena_glyph = glyphs[trecena_start % 20]


    return tone, glyph, trecena_glyph

class Maya:
    @staticmethod
    def calculate_julian_day(day, month, year):
        """Calculate Julian Day Number from Gregorian date."""
        a = (14 - month) // 12
        y = year + 4800 - a
        m = month + 12 * a - 3
        return day + ((153 * m + 2) // 5) + 365 * y + y // 4 - y // 100 + y // 400 - 32045


    @staticmethod
    def convert_to_tzolkin(day, month, year):
        """Convert Gregorian date to Tzolkin date."""
        tzolkin_names = [
            "Ahau", "Imix", "Ik", "Akbal", "Kan", "Chicchan", "Cimi", "Manik",
            "Lamat", "Muluc", "Oc", "Chuen", "Eb", "Ben", "Ix", "Men", "Cib",
            "Caban", "Etznab", "Cauac"
        ]
        julian_day = Maya.calculate_julian_day(day, month, year)
        days_passed = julian_day - 584283  # Tzolkin starting point adjustment
        tzolkin_number = (days_passed % 13) + 1
        tzolkin_name = tzolkin_names[days_passed % 20]


        # Adjusting the Tzolkin number
        adjusted_tzolkin_number = (tzolkin_number + 3) % 13
        adjusted_tzolkin_number = 13 if adjusted_tzolkin_number == 0 else adjusted_tzolkin_number
        return adjusted_tzolkin_number, tzolkin_name


    @staticmethod
    def get_birthdate_and_calculate():
        year = int(input("Enter birth year (YYYY): "))
        month = int(input("Enter birth month (MM): "))
        day = int(input("Enter birth day (DD): "))
        tzolkin_date = Maya.convert_to_tzolkin(day, month, year)
        return tzolkin_date
